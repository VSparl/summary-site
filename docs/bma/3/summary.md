# Differentiation
- - -
## Constant Rule

$$ f(x) = c $$

$$ f'(x) = 0 $$

> This only applies in **sums**. If $c$ is a _factor_, the _constant factor rule_ applies.

- - -
## Power Rule

$$ f(x) = x^n $$

$$ f'(x) = n \cdot x^{n-1} $$

- - -
## Constant Factor Rule

$$ f(x) = c \cdot g(x) $$

$$ f'(x) = c \cdot g'(x) $$

- - -
## Sum Rule

$$ f(x) = g(x) + h(x) $$

$$ f'(x) = g'(x) + h'(x) $$

- - -
## Product Rule

$$ f(x) = u(x) \cdot v(x) $$

$$ f'(x) = u'(x) \cdot v(x) + u(x) \cdot v'(x)$$


- - -
## Quotient Rule

$$ f(x) = \frac{u(x)}{v(x)} $$

$$ f'(x) = \frac{u'(x) \cdot v(x) - u(x) \cdot v'(x)}{(v(x))^2} $$


- - -
## Chain Rule

$$ f(x) = u(v(x)) $$

$$ f'(x) = u'(v(x)) \cdot v'(x) $$


> Here, $u(x)$ is the **outer function** and $v(x)$ is the **inner function**

- - -
## Differentiation of Trigonometric Functions

$$ f(x) = \sin(x) $$

$$ f'(x) = \cos(x) $$

$$ $$

$$ f(x) = \cos(x) $$

$$ f'(x) = - \sin(x) $$

$$ $$

$$ f(x) = \tan(x) = \frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)} $$

$$ f'(x) = 1 + (\tan(x))^2 $$

- - -
## Differentiation of Exponential Functions

$$ f(x) = e^x $$

$$ f'(x) = e^x $$


Other useful properties:

$$ f(x) = e^{-x} $$

$$ f'(x) = -e^{-x} $$


$$ f(x) = x \cdot e^x $$

$$ f'(x) = e^x \cdot (x+1) $$


- - -
## Differentiation of Logarithmic Functions

$$ f(x) = \log_a(x) $$

$$ f'(x) = \frac{1}{\ln(a) \cdot x} $$


> Special case: $f(x) = \ln(x) = \log_e(x) \to \ln(a) = \log_e(e) = 1 \to f'(x) = \frac{1}{x}$
