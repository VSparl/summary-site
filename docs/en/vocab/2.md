| **Unit 20 - Topic Vocabulary: Power** |  |
| aggression | behaviours or actions that are hostile or intended to harm someone or something. |
| authority | the power or right to give orders, make decisions, or control others. |
| benign | gentle in manner; not harmful or likely to cause damage. |
| bully | to repeatedly use strength or influence to intimidate or harm someone weaker. |
| command | to give an authoritative order; to control or direct someone’s actions. |
| conquer | to overcome and take control of a place, people or situation, especially by force. |
| consent | to agree to something or give permission; to allow something to happen. |
| controversy | a prolonged public disagreement or heated discussion. |
| dictator | a ruler who has total power over a country, typically one who has obtained control by force. |
| dominate | to have power or exert control over someone or something; to be dominant. |
| eliminate | to remove or get rid of something completely. |
| enforce | to compel obedience to or ensure observance of laws or rules. |
| entitled | believing oneself to be inherently deserving of privileges or special treatment. |
| exempt | free from an obligation or liability imposed on others. |
| former | having previously been a particular thing; previous. |
| impose | to force something (like a rule, tax, or idea) on others. |
| inferior | lower in status, quality, or value compared with another or others. |
| intimidate | to frighten or overawe someone, especially in order to make them do what one wants. |
| label | to assign a descriptive or identifying word or phrase to someone or something. |
| liberate | to set someone free from a situation, especially imprisonment or oppression. |
| mainstream | belonging to or constituting the dominant trend or majority of a group or society. |
| master | to gain control or become highly skilled at something; to be dominant. |
| minister | a person in charge of a government department or a member of the clergy; to attend to the needs of others. |
| monarch | a sovereign head of state, especially a king, queen, or emperor. |
| prohibit | to officially forbid something by law, rule, or authority. |
| reign | to hold royal office; to be the monarch; to rule. |
| reinforce | to strengthen or support an idea, structure, or behaviour. |
| reluctant | unwilling and hesitant; disinclined to do something. |
| resist | to strive against, oppose or refuse to accept someone or something. |
| restrict | to limit the size, amount, or range of something; to confine. |
| society | a community or group of people living together and sharing norms, laws, and organisations. |
| subject | to bring someone or something under the control or domination of another; to expose to a condition. |
| subjective | based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes, or opinions rather than objective facts. |
| submit | to accept or yield to a superior force or to someone’s will; to present something for consideration. |
| summon | to order someone to come to a place; to call upon someone to be present. |
| superior | higher in rank, status, or quality. |
| undermine | to weaken or damage something, especially gradually or insidiously. |
| unrest | a state of dissatisfaction, disturbance or agitation among a group of people. |
| victimise | to single out someone for cruel or unjust treatment; to make a victim of. |
| vulnerable | able to be easily hurt, influenced, or attacked. |
